# Lodging and Transport

## Lodging Strategy

- Book locations before optimizing hotel brand. The right base saves more family energy than a more famous hotel in the wrong neighborhood.
- Target comfortable, well-located family hotels at USD $200-$250 per night. Do not exceed $250 per night without explicit approval.
- Treat a confirmed single room or suite for all four travelers as a key requirement. Two-room setups are too expensive and should be a fallback only if no acceptable one-room option exists.
- Treat Cambria, Paso Robles, or San Luis Obispo as a fallback stop, not part of the default route.
- Prefer suites, family rooms, or large rooms with confirmed occupancy for all four travelers.
- In Las Vegas, compare the full stay cost, not only the headline room rate. Include resort fees, taxes, parking, pool access, and any extra-person fees.
- Confirm crib/rollaway policy, parking height, resort fees, and cancellation terms before booking.

## Transport Plan

1. Fly Sydney to San Francisco.
2. Stay car-free in San Francisco for the first four nights.
3. Pick up a rental car on Day 5 when leaving San Francisco.
4. Use the car for Yosemite, the Central Coast, Santa Barbara, Los Angeles, and Las Vegas.
5. Return the car at LAS on departure day if the Las Vegas return itinerary is booked.
6. Fly Las Vegas to Sydney via a connecting gateway on Day 18 if the July 1-20 window is fixed.
7. Keep Los Angeles-Sydney return as a quote fallback only if LAS fares, connection times, or one-way car fees are poor.

## Rental Car Requirements

- Book a midsize or full-size SUV with luggage space for four passengers, pram, and long-haul luggage.
- Confirm child seat requirements for the younger child before pickup. Bring or rent an approved seat.
- Confirm whether the rental company requires an International Driving Permit for an Australian license.
- Add a second authorized driver if both adults may drive.
- Price SFO city pickup to LAS airport return before booking. Compare against SFO pickup to LAX return if the family considers returning from Los Angeles.
- Confirm one-way drop-off fees, Nevada return rules, parking fees at each hotel, and whether the Las Vegas hotel has self-parking that fits the vehicle.

## Airport and Transfer Notes

- SFO arrival: Use a car service or taxi to the hotel. Do not collect a rental car on arrival.
- LAS departure: Leave a large traffic buffer for rental car return, luggage, terminal transfer, and the first domestic or international connection.
- Return-flight fallback: LAX-SYD may still be operationally easier if LAS-SYD connecting quotes are expensive or poorly timed.
- Final-day option: Book late checkout or a day room rather than filling the departure day with sightseeing.
