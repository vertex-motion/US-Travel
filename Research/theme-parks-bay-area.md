# Bay Area Theme Parks — Research Paper

<!-- tag: sources -->

Research into theme parks reachable from the family's **Mountain View** base (Days 10–16, July 7–13), with official links for each park and an analysis of which day in that window has the fewest crowds. Decision support for the open question *"Does the family want a theme park day?"* in [`07-checklist.md`](../07-checklist.md).
Date researched: 2026-06-19.
Context: Family of four — two adults (39), a **13-year-old**, and a **3-year-old**. Self-driving (BMW X5). In the Bay Area July 7–13; committed plans are Golden Gate (Thu July 9) and Stanford/tech (Fri July 10); flexible days are **Wed July 8, Sat July 11, Sun July 12**.
Verify live hours, prices, and ride heights close to travel; parks change schedules and pricing often.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

| Question | Answer for this family |
| --- | --- |
| Is there a theme park *in San Francisco*? | **No.** SF city has no major theme park. The parks are elsewhere in the Bay Area — and two are very close to Mountain View. |
| Closest / best all-ages park? | **California's Great America** (Santa Clara) — ~6 miles / 10–15 min from the hotel. Coasters for the teen + a kids' area for the 3-year-old. See Section 2. |
| **Least-crowded day to go?** | **Wednesday, July 8** (a flex day). It's a weekday — Great America's lightest days are Tue–Thu; Saturday is the busiest. **Avoid the Sat 11 / Sun 12 weekend.** See Section 4. |
| Any closure risk? | Great America closes on **World Cup match days at adjacent Levi's Stadium** — but **all Bay Area matches end July 1**, so the July 7–13 window is clear. See Section 4. |
| Doing this *and* Disneyland? | Probably too theme-park-heavy for one trip with a toddler. Treat LA Disneyland and a Bay Area park as **either/or**, not both. |

**Bottom line:** If the family wants a Bay Area park day, **California's Great America on Wednesday, July 8** is the pick — closest, all-ages, weekday crowds, and no World Cup conflict. Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk is the relaxed coastal alternative; Gilroy Gardens suits the 3-year-old most; Six Flags Discovery Kingdom is the teen-thrill option but the furthest.

## 2. The Parks (with links)

| Park | Official site | From Mountain View | Best for | 3-year-old | 13-year-old |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **California's Great America** (Santa Clara) | [sixflags.com/cagreatamerica](https://www.sixflags.com/cagreatamerica) | ~6 mi · 10–15 min | All ages together | Good — Planet Snoopy / KidZville | Good — coasters + RailBlazer/Gold Striker |
| **Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk** (Santa Cruz) | [beachboardwalk.com](https://beachboardwalk.com/) | ~35 mi · ~40 min | Casual seaside, mixed ages | Good — kiddie rides, beach | Good — Giant Dipper, arcade |
| **Gilroy Gardens** (Gilroy) | [gilroygardens.org](https://www.gilroygardens.org/) | ~30 mi · ~35–40 min | Young children | **Best fit** — gentle rides, gardens, splash | Light — may feel young |
| **Six Flags Discovery Kingdom** (Vallejo) | [sixflags.com/discoverykingdom](https://www.sixflags.com/discoverykingdom) | ~50 mi · ~55–70 min | Teens / thrill | Limited — height limits | **Best fit** — big coasters + animals |

Notes:
- **Great America** is essentially next door to the Mountain View hotel — the only park you can reach in well under 20 minutes. Free entry to the land/park requires a ticket; under-2s typically free (the 3-year-old needs a ticket). Hours in summer roughly **10:00 am–6:00 pm or later**.
- **Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk** has **free admission**; you pay per ride or buy an unlimited-ride wristband. Coastal — cooler and often foggy in the morning, clearing by midday. Pairs naturally with a beach afternoon.
- **Gilroy Gardens** is a gentle family/garden park (summer hours ~**11:00 am–5:00 pm**, open daily in season). Strongest for the 3-year-old; the 13-year-old may find it tame.
- **Six Flags Discovery Kingdom** mixes thrill coasters with marine/animal exhibits; furthest and most intense — best if the day is built around the teen.

## 3. Fit for a 3-year-old + a 13-year-old

- **Great America** is the only park that meaningfully serves both on the same day: the teen rides Gold Striker, RailBlazer, and the larger coasters while the 3-year-old has **Planet Snoopy / KidZville** with low- and no-height rides. Use **rider swap** for the big coasters.
- **Santa Cruz** works as a lighter, cheaper, pay-as-you-go day — the toddler does kiddie rides and sand, the teen does the historic **Giant Dipper** and arcade. Less "full theme park," more seaside fun.
- **Gilroy Gardens** flips the balance toward the 3-year-old; only choose it if the priority is the little one.
- **Six Flags Discovery Kingdom** flips toward the teen; the 3-year-old is height-limited on the headline rides.

## 4. Crowd Analysis — Best Day in the July 7–13 Window

**Rule of thumb:** in summer, **weekday << weekend**. For Great America specifically, tracked wait-time data shows **Tuesday is the least busy (~20 min average wait)** and **Saturday the busiest (~34 min)**; Tue–Thu are consistently the quietest. The same weekday-beats-weekend pattern holds for Santa Cruz and the others. Arriving at **opening (rope drop)** matters as much as the day.

Mapping that onto the family's actual days:

| Date | Day | Status | Crowd outlook | Verdict |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tue Jul 7 | Day 10 | Arrival from Monterey (afternoon) | Lightest weekday, but only a partial day | Not enough hours |
| **Wed Jul 8** | **Day 11** | **Flex** | **Low — mid-week** | **★ Best choice** |
| Thu Jul 9 | Day 12 | Committed: Golden Gate | Low, but day is taken | Only if plans reshuffle |
| Fri Jul 10 | Day 13 | Committed: Stanford/tech | Building toward weekend | Only if plans reshuffle |
| Sat Jul 11 | Day 14 | Flex | **Busiest day of the week** | Avoid |
| Sun Jul 12 | Day 15 | Flex | Weekend-heavy | Avoid |
| Mon Jul 13 | Day 16 | Departure to Mammoth | n/a | Travel day |

- **Recommendation: Wednesday, July 8 (Day 11).** It is the one flex day that is also a low-crowd weekday. Of the three flex days, the other two (Sat 11, Sun 12) are exactly the days to avoid.
- **World Cup is not a factor for these dates.** Great America closes on Levi's Stadium match days, but the Bay Area's six matches all fall **on or before July 1**, six days before the family arrives — so there is no closure and no post-match crowd or traffic spillover during July 7–13. (See [`world-cup-2026-matches.md`](world-cup-2026-matches.md).)
- **Heat note:** inland Santa Clara and Gilroy run hot in July; coastal Santa Cruz stays cooler. If July 8 is a heat-spike day, Santa Cruz Boardwalk is the more comfortable swap.

---

## 5. Recommendation

1. If the family wants a Bay Area park day: **California's Great America, Wednesday July 8**, rope-drop start, rider-swap for the teen's coasters, midday break for the 3-year-old.
2. **Either/or with Disneyland:** doing both an LA Disneyland day and a Bay Area park day is likely too much theme-park time for a first trip with a toddler — pick one region.
3. **Cheaper / lighter alternative:** Santa Cruz Beach Boardwalk (free entry, pay-per-ride, beach) on the same Wednesday if a full coaster park feels like too much.
4. Keep the decision **Open** — it is a family-preference call, not a logistics blocker.
