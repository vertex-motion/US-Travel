# Medical / Travel Insurance Research

<!-- tag: sources -->

Research on emergency medical travel insurance for this trip. Scope: emergency
medical cover only — cost and what each policy includes for medical. Cancellation,
baggage, and other extras are out of scope per traveler request.

Date researched: 2026-06-11. All prices are indicative; confirm with a live quote.

## Trip Parameters Used

| Parameter | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Travelers | Family of four: 2 adults (39, 39), teen (13), child (2) |
| Residence | Sydney, Australia (buy an Australian outbound policy) |
| Destination | United States (West Coast) |
| Duration | 28 Jun – 20 Jul 2026, ~23 days |
| Pre-existing conditions | None declared — no extra loading or medical assessment needed |

## Why Medical Cover Matters Most Here

- US hospital costs are among the highest in the world: AUD $10,000–$50,000+ per day is possible, and an air evacuation can exceed USD $50,000.
- For the USA, target **unlimited or at least AUD $1 million** emergency medical cover, plus unlimited medical evacuation/repatriation.
- Children are covered **free** as dependents on a family policy with every Australian insurer below (both the 13- and 2-year-old qualify), so you insure two adults and the kids are added at no cost.

Notes on the table:
- "Unlimited" means no fixed dollar cap on reasonable emergency medical or evacuation costs — the strongest position for the USA.
- All listed insurers add both children (ages 13 and 2) free as dependents, so you only pay two adult premiums. Confirm the dependent age limit in each PDS.
- A non-zero excess (typically AUD $100–$200) lowers the premium; most insurers let you buy the excess down to $0 for more.
- Medical-only tiers (e.g. Fast Cover Medical Only) are the cheapest route if you want medical cover and nothing else. Tick is the value-focused full-policy option.
- World Nomads tends to price higher and is aimed at adventure activities; its Standard plan caps medical at $5M, so choose Explorer for unlimited.
- Bupa, Medibank, and RACV give ~15% member discounts — relevant only if you already hold their health/membership product.

| Travellers | 2 adults (both born 1986/87, age 39) + 2 dependent children (ages 13 and 2) |
| Pre-existing conditions | None |

## What "Emergency Medical" Includes (Typical, USA Comprehensive)

- Overseas hospital and treatment costs for sudden illness or injury.
- Emergency medical evacuation and repatriation to Australia.
- Emergency overseas dental for pain relief.
- 24/7 emergency assistance hotline.
- Repatriation of remains.

## Recommendation

1. For the USA, prioritize **unlimited emergency medical + unlimited evacuation**. Most full policies above qualify; Southern Cross, Fast Cover, Tick, Cover-More, 1Cover, Bupa, Medibank, and RACV all offer unlimited on both.
2. If you want medical cover only and minimal extras, **Fast Cover Medical Only** (unlimited medical/evacuation) is the most cost-targeted option. **Tick Standard/Top** is the cheapest full-policy option with unlimited medical.
3. If you already hold Bupa or Medibank health cover, or RACV membership, get their quote too — the ~15% discount can make an otherwise mid-priced policy competitive.
4. Get three live quotes using the inputs in "Getting Exact Quotes" (e.g. Fast Cover, Tick, Southern Cross). Expect roughly **AUD $250–$450 medical-only** or **AUD $350–$700 comprehensive** for the whole family.
5. Buy soon after the trip is fully booked so cancellation cover (if you later decide you want it) starts; medical cover itself applies from the travel dates.
