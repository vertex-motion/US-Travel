## 2. What Queues to Expect

- **Summer is LAX's heavy season.** LAX has among the **longest customs waits of major US airports**, averaging more than 30 minutes June–August, with peak windows of **45–90 minutes**.
- **The wait is driven by how many wide-body flights land in the same 30-minute window**, not just the time of day. A single 787 is quick; four trans-Pacific jets at once fills the hall.
- **Our slot (~06:05):** the early-morning bank at TBIT is when most flights from Australia and Asia arrive. Expect a busy hall, but also full CBP staffing. This is *not* the worst window (late afternoon/early evening is typically heavier), but it is not empty.
- **Two separate queues exist:** first **immigration** (passport control), then — after collecting checked bags — **customs**. Budget time for both plus the walk and baggage wait.

**Planning number:** assume **~75–90 minutes** from landing to curb (deplane + immigration + bags + customs). Treat anything faster as a bonus.

## 3. The Arrival Process, Step by Step (TBIT)

1. **Deplane** at Terminal B and follow "Arrivals / Immigration" signs to the Federal Inspection Services (FIS) hall.
2. **Immigration — Simplified Arrival.** Join the **"Visitors" / "Foreign Passports"** queue (not the US/Canada lane). At the booth a camera takes each traveler's **photo**; the officer asks a few questions (purpose of trip, where staying, how long). No paper I-94 and no paper arrival card.
   - **Fingerprints:** first-time VWP travelers aged 14+ may have fingerprints taken. The **2-year-old is exempt** (under 14). This adds a little time per adult.
3. **Baggage claim.** Collect all checked bags. Grab a free baggage cart if needed (a pram helps for the toddler; keep it accessible).
4. **Customs.** Hand the officer **one family declaration**, or answer verbally if directed. Most travelers with nothing unusual to declare walk straight out; those with food/agricultural items go to secondary inspection.
5. **Exit** into the TBIT arrivals area, then proceed to the rental-car shuttle / pickup.

## 4. Fast-Track Options — and Why Most Don't Apply

| Option | Available to us this trip? | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Automated Passport Control (APC) kiosks** | ❌ No | The APC kiosk program was **terminated September 30, 2023** and folded into Simplified Arrival. The kiosks are gone. |
| **Mobile Passport Control (MPC) app** | ❌ No | MPC for VWP travelers requires **at least one prior US entry** under the Visa Waiver Program. This is the family's first US trip, so no one qualifies. (Generic travel blogs wrongly recommend it — it will not work for first-timers.) |
| **Global Entry** | ❌ Not in time | Australians **are** now eligible, but the process needs a US application, an Australian background check (A$263.50 adult / A$123.50 under-10), conditional approval (weeks), and an interview or Enrollment-on-Arrival. Cannot be completed before June 28. Worth it for a *future* trip — see Section 7. |
| **Airline premium-cabin priority lane** | ❌ No | Economy booking; no Premier/business priority immigration access. |
| **Simplified Arrival (facial biometrics)** | ✅ Yes (everyone) | This *is* the standard process now. It is faster than the old kiosk-and-fingerprint flow, but it is not a separate "fast lane." |
| **Diplomatic / crew / known-traveler lanes** | ❌ No | Not applicable. |

**Conclusion:** No skip-the-line product is open to this family on this trip. Speed comes from the operational tactics in Section 5.

## 5. Tips and Tricks We Can Actually Use

1. **Deplane fast.** The biggest variable we control is reaching the immigration hall before the surge from our own flight and any flight that lands minutes earlier. If seats are unassigned far back, consider paying to sit **closer to the front** for a quicker exit. Have everyone ready to stand and move the moment the door opens.
2. **Have documents in hand before the hall.** All four **passports** out and open to the photo page; know the **first hotel address** (Pasadena area) and the **return flight** date (UA839, July 18). ESTA approvals are linked to the passports electronically — no printout needed, but keep the ESTA application numbers accessible just in case.
3. **Use the family advantage.** Travelers with **young children** are often waved into a shorter or priority line, or to a dedicated family/wheelchair lane. **Ask a CBP line officer** on arrival whether a family lane is open — it is not guaranteed at LAX, but costs nothing to ask. Keep the pram visible.
4. **Answer questions simply and consistently.** Tourism; staying ~20 days; first hotel in Pasadena; returning to Sydney July 18. Let one adult lead the answers; the officer may address the children briefly.
5. **One family customs declaration.** Members of the same household traveling together file a single declaration. Decide *before* landing whether anyone is carrying food.
6. **Keep the toddler regulated.** Plan for up to ~90 minutes standing/queuing after a long flight: snacks, water, a charged device, and a nappy change before joining the immigration queue. A fed, rested 2-year-old makes the whole pass smoother.
7. **Don't photograph the immigration hall.** Photos/phone use are prohibited in the FIS area and can cause delays.
8. **Pre-load nothing risky.** No fresh fruit, sandwiches with meat, or plant material in carry-ons "for later" — see Section 6.

## 6. Customs — What to Declare (Avoid Secondary Inspection)

Declaring honestly is faster than getting caught; undeclared agricultural items risk fines and a long secondary inspection.

- **Always declare all food**, including snacks, packaged goods, and any items brought from Australia or bought in transit.
- **Prohibited / high-risk:** fresh fruit and vegetables, meat and meat products, many dairy items, plants, seeds, soil. **Eat or bin these before customs** if unsure.
- **Generally fine:** commercially packaged, shelf-stable snacks; baby formula and toddler food (declare them).
- **Alcohol/tobacco:** not relevant — the family does not drink alcohol and carries no tobacco, so no duty-allowance issues.
- **Cash:** amounts of **US$10,000 or more** (per family, combined) must be reported. Below that, nothing to report.
- **When in doubt, declare it.** Verbal declaration to the officer is enough; they decide if anything needs a closer look.

## 7. Recommendation for Future Trips (Not This One)

- **Apply for Global Entry after this trip.** Australians are now eligible with no prior-US-travel requirement. Cost is roughly **US$120 per person** plus the **A$263.50 / A$123.50** Australian background-check fee. Approval takes weeks and needs an interview or Enrollment-on-Arrival.
- **Bonus:** once each person has **one** US entry on record (which this June trip provides), they also become eligible for **Mobile Passport Control** on the next visit — a free app that speeds the declaration step even without Global Entry.
- For a possible second US trip, starting the Global Entry process **2–3 months ahead** would let the whole family use the expedited lane and skip most of the queue described above.

## 8. Downstream Logistics Note

The ~06:05 landing plus a 75–90 minute clearance means the family likely reaches the rental-car area around **07:30–08:00**. The rental car is booked for an **8:00 am LAX pickup** — a *start time*, not a hard deadline, so a slightly later arrival at the counter is fine. Build the airport-to-Pasadena drive plan around an unhurried exit, not a race to the counter.
