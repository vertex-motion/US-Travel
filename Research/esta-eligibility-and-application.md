## 2. What ESTA Is

ESTA is an automated pre-screening system run by U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP). It is **not a visa** and **not a guarantee of entry** — it authorizes a VWP traveler to *board* a U.S.-bound carrier. The final admission decision is made by a CBP officer at the port of entry (LAX, in this case).

- A valid ESTA is mandatory before boarding any air or sea carrier to the U.S. under the VWP.
- It permits stays of **up to 90 days** for tourism or business.
- It allows **multiple trips** during its validity, each capped at 90 days.
- Under the VWP you **cannot extend** your stay beyond the admitted period and **cannot change status** (e.g., to work or study) while in the U.S.

## 3. Eligibility — Does the Family Qualify?

To use ESTA, a traveler must meet **all** of the following. Each is assessed against this family below.

| Requirement | Detail | This family |
| --- | --- | --- |

## 4. Disqualifiers — Read Before Applying

A traveler is **barred from ESTA** (and must instead apply for a B-1/B-2 visa at a U.S. embassy) if **any** of the following is true. These come from the 2015 VWP Improvement and Terrorist Travel Prevention Act and later additions.

1. **Travel-history bar.** Has traveled to or been present in **North Korea, Iran, Iraq, Libya, Somalia, Sudan, Syria, or Yemen on or after March 1, 2011** (limited official-business exceptions exist for some categories).
2. **Dual-nationality bar.** Is a **dual national of Cuba, Iran, Iraq, Sudan, or Syria** (in addition to their VWP nationality).
3. **Prior refusal or removal.** Has been denied a U.S. visa or ESTA that was not resolved, been refused U.S. entry, or been deported/removed.
4. **Prior immigration violation.** Has overstayed a previous U.S. admission, worked without authorization, or otherwise violated U.S. entry conditions.
5. **Certain criminal history.** Convictions involving moral turpitude, drug offenses, or multiple convictions can disqualify.
6. **Communicable disease / other inadmissibility grounds.**

> **Action:** Each adult should mentally run this list before applying. If any answer is "yes" or "unsure," do **not** guess on the form — a wrong "no" can cause problems at the border. Instead seek a B-1/B-2 visa, which takes much longer (Section 8) and would affect this trip's nonrefundable bookings.

For the 13-year-old and the 2-year-old these bars are almost certainly all "no," but the same questions are asked on their applications and must be answered truthfully by a parent.

## 5. The Application — How to Fill It In

Apply at **https://esta.cbp.dhs.gov** (or the official CBP One mobile app). Each traveler needs a separate application; one payment can cover a group, but **every person, including the infant, must have their own approved ESTA**.

### 5.1 What you need on hand (per traveler)
- The **e-passport** (you enter the passport number, issue/expiry dates, issuing country).
- Personal details: full name (exactly as in passport), date and city/country of birth, sex, national ID number if any.
- Home address and contact email/phone.
- **U.S. point-of-contact / address while in the U.S.** — the first hotel works (the Pasadena-area arrival hotel, June 28).
- **Emergency contact** (name, email, phone) — can be someone outside the U.S.
- Employment details (employer name, address, phone) if employed.
- A **credit/debit card or PayPal** for the fee.

### 5.2 The form sections
1. **Applicant / passport information** — biographic and passport data; upload or key-enter is not needed, you type it.
2. **Travel information** — whether you have a U.S. address yet, point of contact, emergency contact. You do **not** need flight numbers, but you can enter them.
3. **Eligibility questions** — a series of yes/no questions covering the disqualifiers in Section 4 (disease, arrests, drug offenses, terrorism, prior visa/entry refusals, the country-travel-history question, and a consent to waive certain review rights). Answer truthfully.
4. **Review & pay** — verify every field (especially passport number and name spelling — errors here are the most common reason an ESTA fails to match at check-in), then pay.

### 5.3 Tips specific to this family
- **Do all four in one sitting** using the "group of applications" option to track them together and pay once.
- For the **2-year-old and 13-year-old**, a parent completes and submits on their behalf; employment = "not employed."
- Use the **first hotel (Pasadena area, check-in June 28)** as the U.S. address.
- **Double-check each passport number** against the physical passport before paying — a single transposed digit means the airline's check-in system won't find a valid ESTA.

## 6. Cost

- **Total: ~US$40.00 per applicant** (≈ **US$160 for four**).
- Reported component breakdown: **$17 travel-promotion fee + $10 operational fee + $13 Treasury contribution = $40** (one fee tracker lists the total as **$40.27** — treat ~$40 as the planning figure and confirm the exact charge on the official site at payment).
- **When charged:** only a small processing portion (reported as ~$4–$10 depending on source) is taken up front / on denial; the bulk is charged **only if the ESTA is approved**. If **denied**, you are charged just the small processing portion, not the full $40.
- This is a **2025 increase**: the fee rose from **$21 to $40** for applications filed **on or after September 30, 2025** (mandated by Public Law 119-21 / H.R.1).
- Pay **only** on esta.cbp.dhs.gov. Third-party "ESTA" sites add service charges of $30–$100+ for nothing extra.

## 7. Timelines — When to Apply and How Long It Takes

| Milestone | Timing |
| --- | --- |
| CBP recommendation | Apply **as early as possible, and no later than 72 hours before departure**. |
| Typical decision | Most applications are decided **within 24 hours**; allow **up to 72 hours**. |
| Real-time approval | **No longer offered** — do not count on instant approval. |
| Payment window | Pay promptly; an unpaid application is not processed. |
| Validity once approved | **2 years**, or until the passport expires, whichever comes first. Multiple entries allowed, each ≤90 days. |

### Application status outcomes
- **Authorization Approved** — cleared to board. (Still not a guaranteed entry; CBP decides at LAX.)
- **Travel Not Authorized** — cannot use the VWP; must apply for a visa instead.
- **Authorization Pending** — under review; re-check status within 72 hours. Do not re-apply.

### What this means concretely (today is 2026-06-16, departure June 28)
There are **12 days** before departure — comfortable, but the task is currently **open**. Apply **now** so that, in the unlikely event of a "Pending" result or a "Not Authorized" outcome, there is still time to react before nonrefundable bookings are at risk. If approved now, the 2-year validity easily covers the whole trip.

---

## 8. If Someone Is Denied (Contingency)

If any traveler gets "Travel Not Authorized," they cannot travel under the VWP and must obtain a **B-1/B-2 visitor visa**:
- Requires a **DS-160 online form, a ~US$185 fee, and an in-person interview** at the U.S. Consulate (in Sydney).
- **Interview wait times can be weeks to months** — this is the scenario that could threaten the June 28 departure and the family's nonrefundable bookings.
- This is precisely why ESTA must be confirmed **before** relying on the already-booked flights, hotels, and car.

---

## 9. Recent Changes Worth Knowing (2025–2026)

- **Fee nearly doubled** to ~$40 (from $21) on September 30, 2025.
- **No more real-time approvals** — the 72-hour window is now the operating assumption.
- CBP **modernized the ESTA website** and pushes the official **CBP One app**; be alert to lookalike third-party sites.
- The separate **I-94 land-border fee** rose to $30, but that does **not** apply here — this family arrives and departs by air through LAX.
