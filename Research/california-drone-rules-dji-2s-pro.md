# California Drone Rules for DJI 2S Pro - Research Paper

<!-- tag: sources -->

Research into recreational drone rules for California travel with a DJI Air 2S or DJI Mavic 2 Pro-class drone.
Date researched: 2026-06-26.
Context: Australian visitor on a private family trip, flying for personal recreation only. No paid work, monetized work, inspections, property sales, volunteering for an organization, or other non-recreational flights.
Use this as a practical briefing, not legal advice. Re-check FAA, B4UFLY / LAANC, TFRs, wildfire status, and local land-manager rules before each flight.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

| Question | Practical answer |
| --- | --- |
| Can a visitor fly a drone in California? | Yes, but only after FAA setup and only where both airspace and the launch/landing land manager allow it. California is permission-first in practice. |
| What applies to a "DJI 2S Pro"? | Treat it as a DJI Air 2S or Mavic 2 Pro until the exact model is confirmed. Both are over 0.55 lb (250 g), so FAA registration, exterior marking, proof of registration, TRUST proof, and Remote ID apply. |
| Do I need a US drone license? | Not for purely recreational flying. If the flight is for work, business, monetized content, volunteering for an organization, property sales, inspections, or another non-recreational purpose, assume FAA Part 107 is required. |
| What must be done before flying? | Register at FAA DroneZone, mark the drone, take TRUST, confirm Remote ID firmware/app status, add the Remote ID serial number to the registration, and use B4UFLY / LAANC before each launch. |
| Where is the biggest "no"? | National Park Service land: Yosemite, Death Valley, Golden Gate National Recreation Area, Alcatraz, Presidio / Marin Headlands areas, and similar NPS units generally prohibit launching, landing, or operating drones. |
| Are California State Parks allowed? | Sometimes. State Parks allow drones unless a superintendent posted order or park classification prohibits them, but wilderness areas, natural preserves, and cultural preserves prohibit motorized equipment. Check each unit and district before flying. |
| What is the trip posture? | Bring the drone only if setup is complete, but expect many scenic stops to be no-drone or not worth the risk. Use it only at a clearly legal, uncrowded, non-wildlife, non-airport-controlled location. |

**Bottom line:** For this trip, the drone is a selective tool, not a default sightseeing activity. The safest plan is: complete FAA setup, use B4UFLY / LAANC before every launch, avoid national parks and city parks, avoid wildlife/coasts/crowds, skip all World Cup areas, and fly only where the land manager clearly allows launch and landing.

## 2. DJI 2S Pro Assumption

There is ambiguity in the model name:

- If this means **DJI Air 2S**, DJI lists takeoff weight as 595 g (1.31 lb).
- If this means **DJI Mavic 2 Pro**, DJI lists takeoff weight as 907 g (2.00 lb).

Both are above the FAA 0.55 lb (250 g) recreational registration threshold. The same practical rules apply either way.

Remote ID:

- DJI Air 2S supports FAA Remote ID with aircraft firmware V02.04.23.10 or later and DJI Fly V1.7.8 or later.
- DJI Mavic 2 Pro supports FAA Remote ID with firmware V01.00.0796 and DJI GO 4 V4.3.60.
- If the aircraft or controller/app is not updated, do not assume it is compliant. Check the DJI app and FAA Declaration of Compliance system before flight.

## 3. Before-Flight Setup

1. **Decide the operating category.** Fly under recreational rules only if the flight is purely for personal enjoyment. If there is any work, business, paid, promotional, monetized, volunteer-for-an-organization, or property-related purpose, assume Part 107 is needed.
2. **Register at FAA DroneZone.** Recreational registration costs $5 and is valid for three years. For a foreign visitor, FAA treats the certificate as recognition of ownership rather than US aircraft registration.
3. **Mark the drone.** Put the FAA registration number on the exterior. Carry proof of registration.
4. **Take TRUST.** The Recreational UAS Safety Test is free online. Carry proof when flying.
5. **Confirm Remote ID.** Update firmware and app. Add the Standard Remote ID serial number or approved broadcast-module serial number in FAA DroneZone.
6. **Install a B4UFLY / LAANC app.** Use an FAA-approved provider such as Aloft, AutoPylot, Airspace Link, Avision, or UASidekick. Use it before each launch.
7. **Check local land permission.** FAA airspace approval is not permission to launch, land, or operate from a park, beach, city property, private land, or attraction.
8. **Check TFRs and wildfire activity.** Do not fly near emergencies, fires, rescue operations, stadiums, World Cup events, or any active FAA TFR.

## 4. Core FAA Rules for Recreational Flights

| Rule area | Practical requirement | Trip action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Purpose | Fly only for personal enjoyment. Non-recreational flights fall under Part 107 unless another FAA path applies. | Do not use the drone for paid, monetized, promotional, inspection, or volunteer-organization work. |
| Visual line of sight | Keep the drone in sight, or use a co-located visual observer in direct communication with the pilot. | No long coastline or mountain "send it out of sight" shots. |
| Aircraft priority | Give way to crewed aircraft and do not interfere. Low aircraft can appear away from airports. | Land or descend early if any helicopter, aircraft, firefighting aircraft, or rescue aircraft appears. |
| Altitude | In uncontrolled Class G airspace, stay at or below 400 ft (122 m) above ground level. In controlled airspace, stay at or below the FAA-authorized altitude. | Do not treat DJI limits as legal limits. Use B4UFLY / LAANC for the actual location. |
| Controlled airspace | In Class B, C, D, or surface Class E airspace, get FAA authorization before flight through LAANC or FAA DroneZone. | Expect controlled-airspace checks around LAX, Hollywood Burbank, Santa Monica, San Francisco, Oakland, San Jose, Palo Alto, Moffett, Monterey, Mammoth, and many heliports. |
| Remote ID | Drones requiring registration must broadcast Remote ID unless flown within an FAA-recognized FRIA. | Keep firmware/app current. If Remote ID fails, land as soon as practicable. |
| Safety | Do not endanger the national airspace system, people, property, or emergency operations. | Treat crowds, busy roads, beaches, playgrounds, and viewpoints as no-drone zones. |

## 5. California and Land-Manager Rules

| Place / rule set | What it means | Trip action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| National Park Service | NPS generally prohibits launching, landing, or operating drones from NPS lands and waters. Violation can be a misdemeanor. | Do not fly in Yosemite, Death Valley, Golden Gate National Recreation Area, Alcatraz, Presidio / Marin Headlands NPS areas, Point Reyes, Mojave National Preserve, or similar NPS units. |
| Yosemite | Yosemite specifically prohibits launching, landing, or operating drones and says it will not issue UAS permits. | No drone in or from Yosemite. Current plan skips Yosemite, but keep the rule. |
| Death Valley | Death Valley prohibits launching, landing, or operating drones except written superintendent approval, usually for extreme or emergency situations. | No drone on the Death Valley day. Heat and wind are additional practical reasons to leave it packed. |
| Golden Gate National Recreation Area | GGNRA is a no-drone zone except two limited locations: Fort Funston when hang gliders/paragliders are not in the air, and Tick Point outside the seasonal closure. | Treat Golden Gate Bridge, Marin Headlands, Alcatraz, Presidio, and coastal viewpoints as no-drone unless a specific GGNRA open site fits. |
| California State Parks | Drones are allowed only where not prohibited by posted order or park classification. State wilderness areas, natural preserves, and cultural preserves prohibit motorized equipment. | Check the exact park unit and district before flying. Avoid state beaches with seals, nesting birds, crowds, or posted orders. |
| California Department of Fish and Wildlife lands | CDFW lands prohibit aircraft, drones, UAS, hovercraft, or hot air balloons except by Special Use Permit. CDFW also flags wildlife-disturbance risk. | Do not fly from ecological reserves or wildlife areas. Keep at least 0.06 mi (0.09 km; 300 ft / 91 m) from wildlife for launch/landing and avoid flying lower than 100 ft (30 m) over wildlife. |
| National forests | Recreational drones can be possible on some National Forest System land, but not from congressionally designated wilderness areas and never near wildfire / TFR areas. | Possible only after checking B4UFLY, wilderness boundaries, local forest orders, fire restrictions, and wildlife/crowd conditions. |
| Los Angeles city parks / Griffith Park | Griffith Observatory states drones or remote-control aircraft are prohibited in all city parks. | No drone at Griffith Observatory, Griffith Park, or LA city park viewpoints. |
| San Francisco Rec & Park | San Francisco Rec & Park says permits are required for any UAS flight on, from, within, or over Rec & Park property. | Do not fly from Golden Gate Park, Dolores Park, or other SF Rec & Park property without permit approval. |
| Private property and privacy | California Civil Code 1708.8 creates liability for recording private, personal, or familial activity in an offensive way, including from airspace in some circumstances. | Do not fly over homes, backyards, pools, hotel windows, patios, or people who could reasonably expect privacy. |
| Emergency scenes | California Penal Code 402 covers drone use that impedes emergency personnel. Government Code 853 protects local agencies/employees from liability for damaging a drone that interferes with emergency services. | Never fly near wildfire, rescue, police, ambulance, helicopter, crash, or road incident activity. |
| Correctional facilities | California Penal Code 4577 prohibits intentionally operating a UAS on or above prison, jail, or juvenile facility grounds without permission. | Avoid prisons, jails, juvenile facilities, and nearby grounds entirely. |

## 7. Trip-Specific Recommendations

### Do not fly at these planned or likely stops

- Yosemite, even if reconsidered.
- Death Valley National Park.
- Golden Gate Bridge / Marin Headlands / Presidio / Alcatraz / most GGNRA sites.
- Griffith Observatory and Griffith Park.
- LA city parks.
- San Francisco Rec & Park property unless permitted.
- State natural preserves, cultural preserves, state wilderness areas, and any state park with a posted order.
- Wildlife-heavy beaches, seal rookeries, nesting-bird areas, and coastal reserves.
- World Cup venues, official event zones, team hotels, base camps, or training sites during restrictions.
- Wildfire, prescribed-fire, rescue, crash, or police/medical incident areas.
- Any airport-controlled location without LAANC / FAA authorization.

### Possible only after live checks

- Non-wilderness National Forest land away from campgrounds, trailheads, visitor centers, wildlife, wildfire operations, and TFRs.
- A California State Park unit where the exact unit and district have no posted order, the area is not a preserve/wilderness/cultural preserve, and staff/conditions do not indicate a conflict.
- Private land where the owner gives permission and airspace is legal.
- Open, uncrowded non-park areas outside controlled airspace, TFRs, wildlife sensitivity, and local restrictions.

### Best practical trip rule

Use a three-green-light test:

1. **FAA airspace green:** B4UFLY / LAANC says the location, time, and altitude are legal.
2. **Land manager green:** the launch/landing spot permits drones.
3. **Common-sense green:** no crowds, homes, wildlife, wind, smoke, emergency activity, stadium event, or privacy concern.

If any light is not green, do not launch.

## 8. Quick Pilot Checklist

Before the trip:

1. Confirm exact DJI model and serial numbers.
2. Update aircraft, controller, and DJI app firmware for FAA Remote ID.
3. Register at FAA DroneZone.
4. Add the Remote ID serial number to the FAA registration.
5. Mark the registration number on the drone exterior.
6. Take TRUST and store proof offline on the phone.
7. Install at least one FAA-approved B4UFLY / LAANC app.

Before each flight:

1. Check B4UFLY / LAANC for the exact pin, time, and altitude.
2. Check FAA TFRs / NOTAMs, especially near World Cup host cities and wildfires.
3. Check local park, city, state, federal, or private land rules.
4. Check wind, visibility, temperature, smoke, and battery health.
5. Scan for people, roads, homes, wildlife, helicopters, and emergency activity.
6. Keep the drone in visual line of sight and stay below the applicable altitude.
7. Land immediately if aircraft, emergency activity, wildlife reaction, staff instruction, Remote ID fault, or crowding creates a concern.
