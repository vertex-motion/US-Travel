## 2. The Selection Filters

A good anchor for *this* family must clear all four:

1. **Toddler admission** — the 3-year-old can enter (free is ideal) and isn't shut out by a height or age minimum.
2. **Teen engagement** — a 13-year-old stays genuinely interested, not bored.
3. **Fully indoor / heat-safe** — July highs run **103–105 °F**; the anchor must be air-conditioned with minimal outdoor exposure.
4. **Toddler-paced** — short, or self-paced so the family can leave when the 3-year-old fades. Long fixed-seat shows fail this.

Cost is the tiebreaker, since the plan books only one anchor and keeps the rest of Vegas free.

## 4. The Two Best Attractions

### Shark Reef Aquarium (Mandalay Bay) — best value, best whole-family fit
- **2026 timed-entry pricing:** adults **$29**, ages 5–12 **$24**, **ages 4 and under FREE**. "Anytime" tickets cost ~$7 more. For this family: 2 adults + teen at $29 ≈ **$87**, **3-year-old free**. Children 12 and under must be with a paid adult.
- **Why it fits:** fully indoor, **self-paced** (leave whenever the toddler fades), a **touch pool** (rays, horseshoe crabs) for the 3-year-old, and sharks, a sea turtle, and a shipwreck tunnel that still engage a 13-year-old.
- **Hours:** hourly timed entries **10:00 a.m.–6:00 p.m.** (last entry). Book a slot that follows the morning pool/Bellagio block and dodges peak afternoon heat.
- **Note:** the optional VR add-on is **not** for ages 4 and under — skip it; the base aquarium is the point.

### Omega Mart at AREA15 (Meow Wolf) — best "wow" and best teen day
- **2026 pricing:** adult (ages 13–99) **$49**; **ages 0–3 FREE**; child (4–12) discounted. For this family ≈ **$147** (2 adults + teen at $49, 3-year-old free).
- **Why it fits:** an immersive, surreal walk-through "supermarket" that opens into hidden interactive worlds — **self-paced**, a standout for a 13-year-old, and colorful/explorable for a 3-year-old. A Certified Autism Center; staff note some areas can be loud/overwhelming, so **bring the toddler's ear protection** if sensitive.
- **Trade-off:** it is **off-Strip** at AREA15 (~10–15 min drive), the only candidate that breaks the Strip-based, no-drive intent of the Vegas days. Worth it for the experience; weigh against the heat-safe convenience of staying on-Strip.

## 5. If They Want a *Show*: Blue Man Group

- **Recommended age 3+.** **Under-3 sits on an adult's lap free**; ages 4+ buy a ticket. This is the key differentiator — it is the **only marquee Vegas show that comfortably admits a 3-year-old.**
- **2026 pricing:** from **~$67–72/person**; for 2 adults + teen ≈ **$200–216**, with the **3-year-old free on a lap**. At the **Luxor** (south Strip).
- **Why it works:** percussion, color, light, and harmless audience interaction — visual and rhythmic rather than dialogue-driven, so a toddler can follow it. Loud, so **bring ear protection** for the 3-year-old.
- **Caveat:** it is still a **~90-minute fixed-seat** show late-ish in the day; have an exit plan if the toddler can't last. Any guest under 18 needs an adult present.

## 6. What to Skip as the Anchor

- **Sphere — *Postcard from Earth*:** a genuine spectacle on the world's largest LED screen, but **recommended age 6+**, intense/loud 4D effects, and **every guest 2+ needs a paid ticket** (~$94–114+ each). For four that is **~$380–450** — the most expensive option, with the 3-year-old as the poorest fit. Consider it only if the family reframes the anchor around the **adults + teen** and accepts the toddler may struggle. Not recommended as the *family* anchor.
- **Cirque du Soleil (Mystère is the most family-friendly):** marketed "all ages," but **any child over age 1 must buy a full ticket**, and it is a **90-minute seated** show — a hard ask for a 3-year-old, at **~$300+** for the group. A child/under-12 discount exists but only **by phone or box office**, not online. Better suited to a return trip when the youngest is older. **Drop for this trip.**
- **High Roller:** **air-conditioned cabins** make it heat-safe and it's cheap (~$80–100; tickets from ~$31 adult day / ~$37 night), but a 30-minute wheel rotation is **too thin to be the day's single anchor**. Keep it as a short **evening add-on** (already listed for the evening Strip block), not the paid anchor.

---

## 7. Recommendation

1. **Default pick — book Shark Reef Aquarium.** Cheapest (~$87), fully indoor, self-paced, free for the 3-year-old, engaging for the 13-year-old. It clears all four filters with the least friction. Reserve a **mid-afternoon timed slot** on July 15 after the morning pool/Bellagio block.
2. **Upgrade pick — Omega Mart** if the family wants a bigger, memorable experience and accepts one short **off-Strip drive**. Best day for the teen; ~$147.
3. **Show pick — Blue Man Group** only if they specifically want a *show*; it is the single marquee show that fits a 3-year-old. ~$200–216; bring ear protection.
4. **In-hotel backup — Madame Tussauds**, inside the Venetian, for a zero-transit, fully heat-proof option or if the booked anchor falls through.
5. **Skip Sphere and Cirque as the anchor** on this trip — both are weak fits for the 3-year-old (cost, age rules, seated duration). Revisit on a future trip.
6. Keep the decision **Open** in the checklist — this is a family-preference call between Shark Reef (value/whole-family), Omega Mart (wow/teen), and Blue Man Group (show), not a logistics blocker. **Book only one.**
