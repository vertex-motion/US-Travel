Yosemite entrance (fee) stations & your route

Enter and pay at Big Oak Flat (Hwy 120); cross the park on Tioga Road; exit at Tioga Pass toward US-395 and Mammoth (no payment on exit). All five entrances are staffed fee stations — there is no free pass-through on this route.

Yosemite entry & fees (2026)Decision: not entering — kept for reference
  • Decision: we skip Yosemite and drive the free Highway 108 / Sonora Pass route around the park, so none of the fees below apply — they are kept here for reference.
  • No entry reservation needed for 2026 — just drive in.
  • Entrance fee: $35 per vehicle (7-day).
  • New 2026 non-resident surcharge: $100 per person aged 16+. The two adults owe $200; the 13- and 3-year-old are exempt. Pay-at-gate Yosemite total is about $235.
  • Recommended: buy one America the Beautiful Non-Resident Annual Pass ($250) — it covers the whole family, waives the surcharge, and also covers Death Valley. Buy at store.usgs.gov.
  • Where you pay: at the Big Oak Flat entrance station (the entry on Hwy 120) — card or phone only, no cash, passports ready. Tioga Pass is the exit, no payment.
  • No free drive-through: the route passes a staffed fee station whether or not you stop. Only a non-Yosemite route (e.g. Sonora Pass) avoids the fee.
  • Tioga Road: open since May 15, 2026 — re-check conditions the morning of Day 16.

Sources: NPS nonresident fees · Yosemite fees · official park map

Road rules: Sydney / NSW vs CaliforniaDriving brief: right side + school buses

Decision: use a conservative California driving mode: right side of road, full stops at stop signs, cautious right turns on red only when legal, full stop for flashing red school-bus lights unless the divided/multilane exception is obvious, U.S.-approved child restraint, mounted phone, zero alcohol before driving.

Rule areaSydney / NSW baselineCalifornia differenceTrip action
Road side & speed units Left side; km/h. Right side; mph. A posted 65 means 65 mph (105 km/h), not 65 km/h (40 mph). Start each drive with the verbal check: "right side, right lane after right turn."
Red lights Left turn on red only where a special sign permits it. Right turn on red is usually allowed after a complete stop unless signed NO TURN ON RED; red arrow means wait. Stop first, check pedestrians/cyclists, then turn only if legal and clear.
Stop signs More roundabouts and give-way controls. Four-way stops are common: first arrival goes; ties yield to the driver on the right. Do not roll stops. Take turns calmly.
School buses Flashing school bus lights usually mean pass/overtake no faster than 40 km/h (25 mph) under NSW rules. Flashing red school-bus lights usually mean stop from either direction until lights stop, except on the far side of a divided or multilane highway. When unsure, stop.
Child restraints Under 7 needs AS/NZS 1754 approved restraint; 145 cm (4 ft 9 in) is the suggested adult-belt height. Under 8, or under 4 ft 9 in (145 cm), needs a federally approved child restraint/booster in the rear seat. Use a U.S.-approved rental or purchased seat; do not rely on an Australian-only approval label.
HOV / express lanes Sydney uses T2/T3 transit lanes, bus lanes, and toll roads by signs/times. California left freeway lanes may be HOV/carpool or HOT/Express lanes. Most require 2+ or 3+ occupants; express lanes may require FasTrak. Do not cross double solid lines. If the whole car is travelling together, occupancy may be fine; still avoid express/toll lanes unless signs and payment are clear.
Alcohol & phones Full licence BAC must be under 0.05; handheld phone use banned and full-licence phone use is limited. Adult BAC limit is 0.08, but DUI can still apply below that if impaired. Handheld phone/texting is illegal; mounted phone can be single-touch. Use zero alcohol before driving and let the passenger handle navigation changes.

Sources checked: NSW road safety and rules · California Driver Handbook · Caltrans HOV

California drone rules for DJI 2S ProBring only if FAA-ready

Decision: bring the DJI only if FAA registration, TRUST, exterior marking, Remote ID firmware/app setup, and B4UFLY / LAANC checks are complete. Treat it as a selective permission-first activity, not a default scenic-stop activity: no national parks, no LA city parks, no World Cup zones, no emergency / wildfire areas, no wildlife-heavy coast stops, and no airport-controlled airspace without FAA authorization.

AreaRule / factTrip action
Drone setup DJI Air 2S and Mavic 2 Pro are both over 0.55 lb (250 g). Recreational flyers must register, mark the drone, carry proof, take TRUST, and comply with Remote ID. Update DJI firmware/app, add the Remote ID serial in FAA DroneZone, carry TRUST + registration proof offline, and check B4UFLY / LAANC before every launch.
FAA airspace Fly only for personal enjoyment, keep visual line of sight, yield to aircraft, stay at or below 400 ft (122 m) in uncontrolled airspace, and get FAA authorization in controlled airport airspace. Expect LA, SF Bay Area, Monterey, Mammoth, and airport/heliport-adjacent spots to need live app checks. DJI unlock status is not legal permission.
No-drone places NPS land generally prohibits launching, landing, or operating drones; Yosemite and Death Valley are explicit no-drone parks. LA city parks prohibit drones. SF Rec & Park requires permits. Do not fly at Yosemite, Death Valley, Golden Gate / Marin Headlands / Presidio / Alcatraz, Griffith Observatory / Griffith Park, LA city parks, or SF parks without permit approval.
State parks California State Parks allow drones only where no posted order or park classification prohibits them; natural preserves, cultural preserves, and state wilderness areas prohibit motorized equipment. Check the exact state park unit and district first. Avoid seal, nesting-bird, crowded beach, Big Sur, and preserve situations unless staff and posted rules clearly allow the flight.
Wildlife / privacy CDFW says drones can disturb wildlife and CDFW lands need a Special Use Permit. California privacy law creates liability for intrusive recording of private, personal, or familial activity. Do not fly over homes, hotel windows, backyards, pools, people expecting privacy, wildlife, rookeries, nesting birds, or ecological reserves.
World Cup / events FAA World Cup 2026 no-drone zones apply to SoFi Stadium on Jun 28, Jul 2, and other LA match days; Levi's Stadium restrictions run through Jul 1. Fan-event restrictions can extend 1.2 mi (1.9 km; 1 NM) from sites and stadium restrictions can extend 3.5 mi (5.6 km; 3 NM). Do not bring or fly the drone near SoFi, Levi's, official World Cup events, team sites, base camps, stadiums, or major events unless current NOTAMs and land rules explicitly allow it.

Sources checked: FAA recreational flyers · California State Parks · NPS uncrewed aircraft · FAA World Cup no-drone zones

Toll roads & electronic tolling~$10.50 total — Golden Gate only

Which tolls the rental car actually hits on the LAX-to-LAX loop, how to pay, and how much. Near-toll-free trip: the only likely charge is the Golden Gate Bridge (~$10.50, southbound only, and only if we cross it). Every toll facility is cashless — no booths or gates to wait at. LA express lanes and Orange County toll roads are optional and avoidable (stay in the free lanes; the I-10/110 lanes carry a ~$491 fine for crossing the double white line). Cheapest plan: skip all express lanes and settle the Golden Gate with a One-Time Payment by license plate (online, up to 30 days before or 48 hours after) instead of the rental company's pay-by-plate fee.
